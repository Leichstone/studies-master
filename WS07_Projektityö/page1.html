Introduction

Baldur's Gate 3 is a role-playing video game developed and published by Larian Studios. It is the third main game in the Baldur's Gate series, based on the Dungeons & Dragons tabletop role-playing system. The game was released in early access in October 2020, with a full release planned for August 2023. Set in the Forgotten Realms universe - the universe known from D&D -, the game features both single-player and multiplayer modes, allowing players to create their own characters or choose from pre-made ones to embark on an epic adventure filled with exploration, combat, and decision-making.

Larian's previous game Divinity: Original Sin II greatly influenced Baldur's Gate 3, as it adopted many of its gameplay mechanics and design philosophies, but they are vastly different. The lack of ground effects in BG3 is a notable change from Divinity: Original Sin II, as the latter heavily relied on environmental interactions for builds, while Baldur's Gate 3 uses a more traditional approach to combat and character progression, focusing on the Dungeons & Dragons ruleset.

In the following list, I will be jotting down some notes about different character builds I would like to try out in my next run.

Build ideas

  • Cleric (Trickery Domain): A support-focused build that excels in illusion based buffs and debuffing the enemies, while also dealing decent damage with Radiant spells, such as Guiding Bolt and Spirit Guardians. The Light Domain is very solid for carrying runs as well, especially with its strong burst of Radiant damage, and the utility of being hit a little less often.
  • Rogue (Assassin): A stealthy and agile build that specializes in pickpocketing, lockpicking, and dealing high damage with sneak attacks right at the start of combat with guaranteed critical hits. I love Assassin for its heavy-hitting attacks. Swashbuckler is a strong alternative which is extremely fun to play and has wonderful utility which can be used each turn.
  • Wizard (Bladesinging): A spellcasting build that focuses on dealing high and consistent damage with physical attacks, boosting one of the highest Armor Class values, especially for a spellcaster. The Bladesinger subclass allows for a mix of melee and ranged combat, where you are able to spend your slots freely on utility and control, or massive area-of-effect damage with evocation spells, such as Shatter, Lightning Bolt, Fireball, and Wall of Fire. This build would be perfect for players who enjoy a more offensive playstyle, as well as a focus on spellcasting and magical abilities.
  • Fighter (Arcane Archer) / Barbarian (Giant): A straightforward and durable build that excels in melee combat with a two-handed weapon, utilizing the Great-Weapon Master (GWM) feat, allowing for huge bursts of consistent damage every turn. The Arcane Archer subclass provides additional ranged options and magical effects, while the Giant subclass increases overall survivability. This build would be great for players who enjoy a more tank-like role, as well as a focus on physical combat and weapon mastery.
  • Final notes: It's good to have your main character, usually referred to as "Tav", have decent Charisma and skills supporting that Ability Score as well. I much prefer to control one character around the map without having to swap to a different character just to talk to someone. That aside, the other classes I have been interested in would be Druid, Ranger, and Bard, as I have yet to properly play those classes to their fullest.
