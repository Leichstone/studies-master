Introduction

This page is about Warhammer 40k: Darktide, a cooperative horde shooter developed by Fatshark. The game is set in the Warhammer 40,000 universe, and is a spiritual successor to Vermintide 2, Fatshark's previous game set in the Warhammer Fantasy universe.

Here I will show off a loadout for the Psyker class I have come up with recently, and some notes on how it works effectively. The differences between loadouts/builds in the different games presented here are quite significant which I hope to illustrate, as well as the differences between Vermintide 2 and Darktide within the same genre.

The above talent tree showcases Bounty Hunter's ranged talent selections in Vermintide 2. On top of this, classes have 1-3 unique passive talents that are automatically active, and an active ability that can be used with a cooldown.

The talents are generally easy to understand and at worst, they're niche choices that don't significantly impact gameplay most of the time. Here's a few examples of skill functions:

  • Open Wounds: Critical hits cause enemies to take 20% increased damage for 15 seconds. Does not stack with similar effects.
  • Cruel Fortune: Reduces cooldown on Blessed Shots to 6 seconds.
  • Increases total Power Level by 7.5%. This is calculated before other buffs are applied.


This pales in comparison to Darktide's talent tree, which I won't be showcasing as an HTML element here due to its complexity, so I will use an online skill tree editor instead:

There are a few non-negotiable skill choices here that are just simply too good not to pick, such as Psykinetic's Aura, Quietude, and the Venting Shriek - Warp Siphon combo. Other than that, there are a lot of situational choices to be made on the weapon preferences, and what kind of playstyle one wishes to pursue. Here I am focusing on the Surge staff, which has some stagger combined with high elite damage potential. Another less popular choice would be to use Scrier's Gaze as the active ability, which is more focused on a pure aggressive playstyle.
